# Ecommerce AI Search Optimization: What Citation and AI Traffic Patterns Across 5 Subverticals Tell Us About Going Beyond PDPs and PLPs

- Published: 2026-05-15
- Collected: 2026-06-13

## Summary
A data-led analysis (using Semrush Enterprise AIO and Similarweb data) of AI citation
and Gen AI traffic patterns across five US ecommerce subverticals: general marketplaces,
beauty & skincare, fashion & apparel, consumer electronics, and sports & outdoors.

Key findings:
- AI citations go well beyond product/category pages — guides, support articles,
  size/fit pages, policies, and store locators are frequently cited
- Third-party sources (YouTube, Reddit, marketplaces, social platforms) recur across
  all verticals, but play different roles depending on category
- Each subvertical has a distinct "buyer uncertainty" pattern that should shape content priorities
- AI citation visibility and actual Gen AI traffic are different signals — citations
  show the "evidence layer," traffic shows the "click layer"
- Even category-leading retailers hold only a minority share of citations about themselves,
  making AI visibility largely an off-site corroboration problem

## Why this is relevant
Provides a concrete, vertical-specific framework for what content types (guides, support
pages, size/fit content, policies) actually earn AI citations and traffic — directly
informs content production priorities for AI-powered SEO strategies, especially for
ecommerce-focused content.
