## Content
Breaks down what AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) shares with SEO vs what is new.

Shared between SEO and AEO:
- Technical accessibility (crawl, render, parse, understand)
- Clear information architecture
- Content quality (thin/vague content fails in both)
- Authority signals (links, mentions, citations)
- User intent matching

New in AEO:
- Rank matters after inclusion: in shortlists first still wins; in narrative answers
  mention quality matters
- Mentions can rival links: reviews, comparisons, forums, articles shape the answer
- Prompts are ~5x longer than keywords and harder to target page by page
- Positioning becomes machine-interpreted: AI infers category, competitors, use cases
  from the broader web
- Reputation gets compressed: owned content, third-party coverage, reviews, and
  consensus collapse into one answer

## Notes
One of the clearest practitioner breakdowns of SEO vs AEO differences — directly
relevant to AI-powered content production strategy. The "reputation compression" point
is particularly important for understanding how AI evaluates content.
