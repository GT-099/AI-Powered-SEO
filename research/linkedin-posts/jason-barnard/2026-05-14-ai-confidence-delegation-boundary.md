## Content
Argues entrepreneurs are competing for AI confidence, not clicks. When someone
asks ChatGPT, Gemini, or Perplexity for a recommendation, AI decides before
the brand appears: Can this brand be trusted? Is their information consistent?
Will recommending them make AI look smart or risky?

Introduces the "delegation boundary" concept: the moment a user hands the
decision to the machine. Three different user behaviors:
- Want to compare options
- Want a shortlist
- Want it done (while they grab coffee)

What actually matters for AI confidence:
- AI clearly understands what your brand does
- Information is consistent everywhere
- Credible sources back up what you say
- Sentiment around you is mostly positive

Every accurate mention, consistent fact, and corroborated story builds future
confidence signals — it compounds. The question shifts from "How do I rank?"
to "When AI makes a decision on behalf of my customer, does it trust my brand
enough to say my name?"
