## Content
Argues that annotation is the single gate that decides if content has a chance
in AI search. Being indexed doesn't mean being understood — AI can crawl and
store a page yet still misinterpret what it says. First annotation sets the
baseline and is very hard to fix later.

Personal example: two of Barry Schwartz's articles were attributed to Jason
because a bot misread his author bio — illustrating how mislabeling cascades
into failure.

Key takeaway: don't focus on keywords or links first. Focus on clear, unambiguous
signals AI can confidently annotate — topic, entity, claims. Use standard
terminology, reference known entities, and make it obvious within the first
100 words.

## Notes
Foundational framework for AI-powered SEO content production — "annotation
before publication" is a concrete pre-publishing checklist item that changes
how content is structured and written from the ground up.
