## Content
Clarifies the concept of "chunking" in AI search and content strategy — argues
it is not about pleasing algorithms but about structuring content for real human
readers and intelligent retrieval by modern systems.

Key points:
- Chunking improves relevance in retrieval-augmented systems by enabling
  passage-level matching and better semantic understanding
- Good structure and UX are not mutually exclusive with machine-friendly content
- The web is shifting from "Google-shaped" to "agent-shaped" — machines now
  recombine content across sources, so content must be legible at the passage
  level, not just the page level
- Better-structured content helps agents retrieve and synthesize more
  meaningfully, giving visibility across AI interfaces beyond classic search
- Chunking is not a hack — it is part of future-ready content engineering
