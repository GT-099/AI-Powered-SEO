## Content
Argues that "cloaking" — long a dirty word in SEO — needs to be reconsidered
for AI crawlers specifically. AI crawlers are not search engines: they don't
follow the same rules, don't render like Googlebot, don't have the same value
exchange, and in many cases send no meaningful traffic while consuming original
research, data, and frameworks brands spent real money creating.

Key argument: the implicit web bargain ("you crawl my content, you send me
traffic") has been broken by AI. Technical SEO needs to evolve from "make
everything crawlable" to "make the right things accessible to the right systems
for the right reasons."

Practical implications:
- Content needed for AI Overview/ChatGPT/Perplexity citations should be
  accessible
- Proprietary benchmarks, datasets, internal research, and original frameworks
  deserve differential access controls
- Technical SEOs need to think about rendering, user agents, markdown
  accessibility, and what AI systems can and cannot actually see
