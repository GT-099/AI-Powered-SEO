## Content
Live experiment with Google AI Mode's Personal Intelligence: asked for desk accessory
recommendations without providing a photo or description. Google pulled from his Google
Photos (photos from June 2023 and December 2021) to infer his office aesthetic and
gave solid recommendations based on stale context.

Key insights:
- Google Personal Intelligence infers context users never explicitly provide
- Personalization depends on what Google can access, how it interprets data, and
  whether that data still reflects reality
- Stale personal data can produce confidently outdated recommendations

Implications for content strategy:
- Brands need to be legible across the web through complementary products, partnerships,
  brand mentions in emails, reviews, media, videos, and creator content
- Partnership marketing, collaborative content, digital PR, and entity-level brand
  visibility matter in AI search
- The query is no longer just "best desk accessories" — it becomes a conversation
  incorporating the user's entire digital life context
