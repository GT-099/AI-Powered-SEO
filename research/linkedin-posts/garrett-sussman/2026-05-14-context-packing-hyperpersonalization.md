## Content
Argues SEOs should obsess over context packing and hyperpersonalization. References
Google's Personal Intelligence concept — finding the right pieces of personal data
(Gmail, Photos, Search history, YouTube, saved places, past conversations, receipts)
and putting them into the model's working memory to shape AI output.

Google's own documented limitations of personalization:
- Overpersonalization: one signal becomes the whole personality of the answer
- Mistaken preferences: buying for others corrupts personal profile
- Incomplete information: model acts like it has the whole story from fragments
- Timeline confusion: struggles with past/present/future in personal history
- Relationship confusion: names/emails/photos can lead to wrong human read
- Missing life changes: life changes faster than model's understanding
- Bad assumptions: a receipt can make AI think you only want one brand forever
- Ignored corrections: model may revert after being corrected
- Speed vs depth tradeoff: more personal answers require more processing
- Multimodal risk: wrong context with a screenshot attached

Key implication: brands need to earn their way into the context layer before the
search happens — through emails, newsletters, reviews, communities, videos, receipts,
support interactions, third-party mentions, saved lists, and past purchases.
