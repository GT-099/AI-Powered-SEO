## Content
Argues nostalgia for the old web is a trap for SEOs. Uses a personal example from
SMX Advanced Boston — asking ChatGPT for toddler-friendly Boston activities with
specific constraints (already did swan boats, kid got airsick) and getting a genuinely
useful, contextual answer (the Pride parade) that traditional search listicles couldn't
have provided.

Key argument: AI Search creates real problems for publishers and brands, but for
consumers it often produces faster, more contextual answers. Content strategy needs
to adapt accordingly:
- Commodity content has less value
- Non-commodity content needs to be more specific, harder to create, and higher quality
- Work needs to be more connected to real customer situations

## Notes
Strong framing piece for AI-powered SEO content production — articulates clearly why
generic content is losing value and what "non-commodity" content looks like in practice.
