## Content
Argues there are two internets: the surface layer humans see (websites, copy, design,
storytelling) and the deeper layer AI reads — where buying decisions are quietly being
made. When someone asks ChatGPT "who's the best [category]?", your homepage, creative
campaigns, and white papers don't show up or get cited.

Key message: "Build once for humans. Build deeper for machines."

The question is whether marketers are building for both internets or still designing
for just one.

## Notes
One of Ross's most-shared conceptual frameworks — the "two internets" framing
directly challenges how AI-powered SEO content production should be approached.
Content needs to be built for machine readability, not just human appeal.
